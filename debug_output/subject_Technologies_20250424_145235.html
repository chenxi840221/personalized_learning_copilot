Eat The Invaders

Host Tony Armstrong — with the help of scientists, land carers, an artist and a chef — investigates different invasive species across Australia and the various strategies being used to manage their populations. goes here

Eat the Invaders: Feral cats

Tony Armstrong visits Yookamurra Sanctuary in South Australia, a dedicated safe haven for some of the country's most threatened species to speak with wildlife ecologist and ranger Dr Aly Ross about feral cats.
Duration: 2 minutes 2 seconds2m 2s

Eat the Invaders: New food industries

Tony Armstrong investigates the possibility of creating a sustainable camel meat industry in Australia as a solution to controlling their population numbers. 
Duration: 1 minute 55 seconds1m 55s

Eat the Invaders: Rabbits

Tony Armstrong and CSIRO scientist Dr Tanja Strive discuss the introduction of rabbits to Australia, why they are classified as an invasive species and the impacts they are having on the environment.
Duration: 1 minute 46 seconds1m 46s

Eat the Invaders: The taste test

Tony Armstrong interviews children in Winchelsea, Victoria, about their thoughts on eating rabbit, and he keeps them company while they taste rabbit for the first time!
Duration: 2 minutes 27 seconds2m 27s

Eat the Invaders: Deer

Tony Armstrong is in Tasmania where he speaks to Professor Chris Johnson from the University of Tasmania about the complexity of managing wild deer populations in Australia.
Duration: 2 minutes 14 seconds2m 14s

Eat the Invaders: A feast at MONA

At the Museum of Old and New Art in Tasmania, artist Kirsha Kaechele and chef Vince Trim prepare a feast made of carp.
Duration: 1 minute 2 seconds1m 2s

Eat the Invaders: Cane toads

Tony Armstrong discusses why cane toads are considered an invasive species in Australia. In this video, he visits a group of women in Boonah, Queensland, who dedicate their free time to catching cane toads to control their population.
Duration: 57 seconds57s

Eat the Invaders: Camels

In central Western Australia, Tony Armstrong speaks with cattle farmer Jack Carmody about controlling the wild camel population at his family's farm, Prenti Downs Station.
Duration: 54 seconds54s

Eat the Invaders: The Kiwirrkurra Rangers

Tony Armstrong visits one of the most remote communities in Australia: Kiwirrkurra, in the Gibson Desert to learn First Nations practices of invasive species management from the Kiwirrkurra rangers — the greatest feral cat hunters on the planet!
Duration: 3 minutes 8 seconds3m 8s

Eat the Invaders: Carp

In this video, Tony visits Coorong (Kurangk) in South Australia. He speaks to commercial fisherman Glen Hill about carp while fishing on the Murray River.
Duration: 1 minute 34 seconds1m 34s
